Pyber Ride Sharing



Brief intro:


In light of the continued success of ride sharing app I decided to test my data science skills using ride sharing data. I will be using matplotlib, numpy and pandas as my tools. This project has importing and reading csv files, dataframe analysis, visualizations such as bubble plot and pie charts.





Bubble Plot


The chart to left provides a lot of information in a bubble chart format. First off we can see from the x-axis that urban areas tend to have more rides, followed by suburban and finally by rural. Next the y-axis informs us that the average fare is higher for rural areas, next suburban and lowest for urban areas. There appears to be a relationship between average fare and number of rides. The more rides there are the lower the average fare which we woudl expect. The final piece of information the chart gives us is the number of drivers per city and per city type. As we would expect, there are more drivers in ubran areas and the least amount in rural areas.





Pie Chart #1




This pie chart shows the total number of drivers per city type. As we can see and would expect there are a lot more drivers in the urban areas than there are in the suburban or rural areas. I have broken out the suburban and rural areas to show just how many more there are in the urban areas and that the suburban and rural combined do not come close to the number of drivers in the urban areas.
